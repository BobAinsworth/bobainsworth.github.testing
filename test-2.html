Croatia – A 20-Night Journey Through the Dalmatian Coast

Split (6 nights) → Hvar Town (4 nights) → Korčula Town (4 nights) → Dubrovnik (6 nights)

Split — 6 Nights

Ancient Rome meets lively Mediterranean seafront: café chatter under palm trees on the Riva, golden‑stone alleys tucked inside Diocletian’s Palace, sunsets over Marjan Hill, and a friendly, local vibe.

Top Experiences

  • Diocletian’s Palace & Peristil: wander Roman cellars, cathedral bell tower, courtyards, and hidden alleys.
  • Marjan Park: pine‑scented trails, sweeping viewpoints, hidden swim coves like Kašjuni and Ježinac.
  • Riva Promenade: morning espresso, evening passeggiata, and gelato under the palms.
  • Day trips: Trogir’s UNESCO old town, Klis Fortress for views, Krka or Plitvice waterfalls, Blue Cave & Pakleni isles by boat.

Easy 6‑Day Flow

  • S1: Old Town immersion — Palace tour & bell tower; Riva at sunset.
  • S2: Marjan viewpoints by foot or e‑bike; swim at Kašjuni; seaside dinner.
  • S3: Trogir & Klis Fortress (half‑day each); wine bar evening.
  • S4: Waterfalls day — Krka (boardwalks) or Plitvice (emerald lakes).
  • S5: Island boat — Blue Cave / Vis / Brač, lazy swims and coves.
  • S6: Markets (Pazar & fish market), beach time at Bačvice or Firule, slow final night.

Food & Wine

  • Traditional konobas: Konoba Fetivi, Villa Spiza (Dalmatian pasta, grilled fish).
  • Modern & view spots: Bokeria, Zoi on the Riva.
  • Wine bars: Paradox — try Plavac Mali reds and Pošip whites.
  • Sweet stops: Luka Ice Cream & Cakes; morning burek from a pekara (bakery).

Hvar Town — 4 Nights

A glossy harbor ringed by café terraces, medieval lanes climbing to the Venetian Fortica (Španjola), lavender‑scented hills, and a social vibe that blends chic beach clubs with quiet island sunsets.

Top Experiences

  • Fortica (Španjola) Fortress: golden‑hour overlook of the harbor and Pakleni Islands.
  • Pakleni Islands: taxi‑boat to Palmizana/Ždrilca/Mlini for turquoise coves and long seaside lunches.
  • St. Stephen’s Square (Pjaca) & Cathedral: marble plaza buzzing from espresso to nightcaps.
  • Wine & village loop: Brusje, Velo/Malo Grablje — stone hamlets, lavender roads, family wineries.

Easy 4‑Day Flow

  • H1: Old Town wander — harbor, cathedral, boutiques; sunset at Fortica.
  • H2: Pakleni Islands beach day; lunch at Zori or Laganini; evening gelato on the quay.
  • H3: Island interior — lavender fields, Malo Grablje; tasting of Bogdanuša/Plavac Mali.
  • H4: Extra cove day or brunch in town; swim at Pokonji Dol or Hula Hula.

Food & Wine

  • Harbor favorites: Gariful (seafood), Dalmatino (grill & steak), Fig (creative small plates).
  • Pakleni lunch: Laganini or Zori — feet‑in‑the‑sand dining.
  • Local grapes: Bogdanuša and Prč (whites), Plavac Mali (robust red).
  • Sunset sips: Waterfront lounges; late‑night at Carpe Diem if desired.

Korčula Town — 4 Nights

A pocket‑sized medieval peninsula nicknamed “Little Dubrovnik”: lantern‑lit lanes, tiny stone stairways, tranquil sea promenades, and vineyard‑dotted hills leading to sandy coves.

Top Experiences

  • St. Mark’s Cathedral & Town Museum; Marco Polo lore and rampart views.
  • Lumbarda wine road: bike to Bire, Popić, Cebalo; sip crisp Grk and Pošip.
  • Škoji islets by taxi‑boat: Badija’s monastery and deer, Vrnik’s stone‑quarry village.
  • South‑coast bays: Pupnatska Luka and Vaja — glass‑clear pebbly coves.

Easy 4‑Day Flow

  • K1: Old Town ramparts & cathedral; sunset aperitivo on the sea walls.
  • K2: Lumbarda bike & beach day — tastings + Pržina/Bilin Žal (sandy).
  • K3: Islet‑hopping to Badija & Vrnik; swims and monastery strolls.
  • K4: South‑coast bays; long seaside lunch; evening wine bar in town.

Food & Wine

  • Sea‑view dining: Filippi, LD Terrace (Michelin‑star), Adio Mare for classics.
  • Wineries: Bire (Grk), Popić, Cebalo in Lumbarda; Korta Katarina across on Pelješac.
  • Sweet breaks: gelato on the square; olive oil tastings in local shops.

Dubrovnik — 6 Nights

A dramatic, walled city rising from the sea: burnished limestone streets, terra‑cotta roofs, Baroque facades, and the constant glint of the Adriatic beyond the battlements.

Top Experiences

  • Walk the City Walls at opening or late afternoon for sweeping sea‑to‑rooftop panoramas.
  • Mount Srđ cable car: sunset vistas and the Imperial Fort museum at the top.
  • Old Town essentials: Stradun, Rector’s Palace, Franciscan Monastery & Pharmacy, Gundulić market.
  • Island escapes: Lokrum (10‑min boat) for pines, peacocks, rocky swims; Elaphiti cruise for beaches.

Easy 6‑Day Flow

  • D1: City Walls + Old Town highlights; cable car at sunset.
  • D2: Lokrum island swim day; evening wander of hidden lanes.
  • D3: Elaphiti Islands cruise; beach lunch and coves.
  • D4: Pelješac wine & Ston walls/oysters or Konavle countryside lunch.
  • D5: Museums & market; afternoon swim at Sveti Jakov or Danče.
  • D6: Sea‑kayak under the walls or Mljet National Park day trip; farewell dinner.

Food & Wine

  • Sea‑view splurges: Nautika, 360 Dubrovnik (Michelin‑star).
  • Beloved classics: Proto (seafood), Taj Mahal (Bosnian), Kopun (local dishes).
  • Wine bars: D’Vino; try Malvasija Dubrovačka and Pelješac Plavac Mali.
